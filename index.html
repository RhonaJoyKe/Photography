Elephants are the largest land animals on Earth.Elephants, regardless of how big they are, are also startled by things that move by them fast, like mice.

Lions are the only cats that live in groups. A group, or pride, can be up to 30 lions, depending on how much food and water is available. Female lions are the main hunters. While they're out looking for food, the males guard the pride's territory and their young.

Wolves are the largest members of the dog family.
Wolves prefer to eat large animals like deer, elk, and moose.

Tigers are the largest amongst other wild cats.A punch from a Tiger may kill you.Tiger cubs are born blind and only half of the cubs survive

Zebras do not have stripes on their bellies, and those on their head and body are narrower than the ones on their rump. Even within each species, no two zebras have the same stripes; they are as unique as fingerprints.

Buffalo live mostly on the plains, but some are known to inhabit river valleys as well as forests. Plains Indians used every part of the buffalo to help them survive. Their hides were used for clothing, blankets and shelters, meat was used for food, and bones were used for tools and toys. Horns were used for cups and spoons while tendons were used for thread and bowstrings. Buffalo chips were used for fuel.

There are five species of Rhinos.THey have poor vision.Two African – black and white rhinos – and three Asian – greater one-horned, Sumatran and Javan rhinos. Three of these (black, Sumatran and Javan) are listed as ‘critically endangered’ by IUCN – there are thought to be fewer than 70 Javan and 100 Sumatran rhinos left in the wild, meaning their populations are truly under threat of extinction.
